Explore Bihar's popular food.

Most Popular Food

Litti Chokha

It consists of wheat and sattu with spices, kneaded into round spicy balls, dipped in ghee.Chokha is prepared by mashing boiled vegetables, adding spices and chopped onion, garlic etc and served with Litti as a complimentary delicacy.

Naivedyam

Original roots of Naivedyam can be traced back to Tirupati Balaji Temple of Andhra Pradesh. It contains besan or gram flour, sugar, cashew, raisin, Green cardamoms, Kashmiri-saffron and other flavorings, cooked in ghee and molded into a ball shape.

Chandrakala/ Pedakiya

Very similar to Gujia, Stuffed with sweetened khoya, coconut, cardamom powder and dry fruits in a crispy covering and dipped in sugar-syrup, Chandrakala is a fdish from the traditional food of Bihar that is enough to sweeten your senses!

Chana Ghugni

Chana Ghughni is a spicy-tangy evening snack item from the food of Bihar. Extremely common yet equally delicious. Boiled chickpeas, fried with onion and spices along with “Chuda ka bhuja” (flattened rice) makes it a perfect answer to satisfy your hunger!

Khaja

Khaja is believed to be a 2000 year old preparation, very similar to the Baklava of Ottoman Empire. The crispy dessert is made from wheat flour, sugar, mawa; deep fried in oil. This unique dessert from Bihar is wafery in texture and yet melts in the mouth.

Khajuria/Thekua

The mixture of wheat flour and jaggery is deep fried and lo! This lip-smacking snack is ready to fulfill those hunger attacks! One can also use rice flour instead of wheat flour and suger instead of jaggery to make different varieties.

Malpua

Its batter is a mixture of flour, milk, mashed bananas and sugar which is deep fried in ghee and dipped in sugar syrup. The crispy crust and soft centre melts in mouth, making it all-time favourite delicacy of Bihar.

Balushahi

A sweet, porous flour cup, enclosing a litany of flavours in the Khoya inside – that’s the Bihari food dish, the Balushahi for you. Every time you bite into one, you don’t just taste a sweet. You taste the love of the wonder, that is the food of Bihar.

Parwal ki Mithai

Biharis sure seem to have a sweet tooth. Yes you read that right, they even turned the plain old vegetable like parwal into a sweet dish. The inner pulp of the parwal is scooped out, boiled, dipped in syrup and filled with sweet khoya preperation.

Puri Sabzi

The most common format of Poori there is tagged with the gobi Aaloo sabzi which is mashed potato with a delightful concoction of spices and herbs. The Bihar version of Poori is not complete without the special dessert of jalebi.

Bihar is a place full of history and spiritual significance. Located in the eastern part of India, this state is known as the land of monasteries. There are many things that make this place worth visiting.
